About

I am a doctoral student in linguistics, mainly working on formal syntax and semantics. In particular, my thesis (supervised by Prof. Mary Dalrymple and Prof. Ash Asudeh) is about multiword expressions (such as idioms like pull strings or cut the mustard) and their representation in the lexicon.

My work mostly uses Lexical Functional Grammar, a constraint-based (non-derivational) grammatical framework.

I am also interested in sociolinguistics, and especially in the intersection of language, gender, and sexuality. I have done some work in this area using critical discourse analysis, and hope to do more.

Outside of linguistics, I enjoy techy things, strategy gaming, and being what the alt-right would disparagingly call a social justice warrior.
